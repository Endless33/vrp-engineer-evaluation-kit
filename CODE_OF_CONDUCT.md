# Code of Conduct

# Our Goal

The VRP Engineer Evaluation Kit exists to encourage professional, evidence-based engineering collaboration.

Everyone participating in this repository is expected to contribute respectfully and constructively.

---

# Expected Behavior

Participants are encouraged to:

- communicate professionally;
- support technical statements with evidence;
- respect different engineering viewpoints;
- help improve documentation and reproducibility;
- report issues responsibly;
- focus discussions on engineering.

---

# Unacceptable Behavior

The following behaviors are not appropriate:

- personal attacks;
- harassment;
- discrimination;
- abusive language;
- spam;
- intentional misinformation;
- disruption of technical discussions.

---

# Engineering Discussions

Engineering disagreements are expected.

They should be resolved through:

- reproducible experiments;
- documented observations;
- technical reasoning;
- measurable results.

Evidence should always take precedence over opinion.

---

# Repository Scope

This repository is intended for engineering evaluation.

It is not a forum for personal disputes or non-technical debates.

Please keep discussions focused on:

- validation;
- testing;
- reproducibility;
- engineering improvements.

---

# Enforcement

Contributors who repeatedly violate these principles may have their comments, issues, or pull requests removed to maintain a productive engineering environment.

---

# Final Principle

Professional engineering begins with respect.

Good engineering is strengthened by reproducible evidence.

Thank you for contributing to an open and constructive engineering community.
